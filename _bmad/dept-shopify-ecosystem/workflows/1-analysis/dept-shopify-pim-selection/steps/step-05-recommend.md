# Step 5: PIM Selection Recommendation

## Objective

Synthesize complexity analysis, platform evaluation, and integration assessment into a final PIM recommendation with implementation plan and cost analysis.

## Instructions

Create a final recommendation document following the structure below.

### Recommendation Document Structure

#### Executive Summary (½ page)

- Business context: Revenue, SKUs, key drivers
- PIM complexity score: X/100 with interpretation
- Recommended platform: [No-PIM | Bluestone | Akeneo | Inriver]
- Primary rationale (1-2 sentences)
- Expected outcomes (time to value, cost, capability uplift)

#### Section 1: Complexity Analysis Summary (½ page)

- PIM complexity score breakdown (show scoring across 6 dimensions)
- Interpretation: What score means for platform needs
- Which platforms are "right-sized" for this complexity level

#### Section 2: Platform Comparison (1 page)

Scorecard table showing:

| Platform | Complexity Fit | Integration | Cost | Timeline | Overall Fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Approach | Score (1-5) | Score (1-5) | Score (1-5) | Months | Score (1-5) |

Add narrative explaining:
- Why recommended platform wins on key dimensions
- Why other platforms were considered but rejected
- Tradeoffs accepted in this recommendation

#### Section 3: Selected Platform Deep Dive (1 page)

**Platform**: [Selected platform name]

**Why This Platform**:
- Aligns with complexity score
- Addresses business requirements
- Suitable integration pattern
- Best cost/value for business needs

**Core Capabilities**:
- Attribute management: Describe how it handles product data
- Multi-channel: Which channels supported
- Localization: How it handles languages/regions
- Governance: Workflow and approval capabilities
- Rich media: DAM and asset management

**Key Features Relevant to Business**:
- Feature 1: How it addresses specific business need
- Feature 2: Competitive advantage
- Feature 3: Risk mitigation

**Vendor & Support**:
- Vendor company and stability
- Support model (SaaS, self-hosted, managed services)
- Partner ecosystem and integrators
- Customer base and case studies

#### Section 4: Implementation Roadmap (½ page)

**Phase 1: Setup & Configuration** (Weeks 1-4)
- Vendor onboarding and access setup
- System configuration and customization
- Attribute taxonomy definition
- User setup and permissions

**Phase 2: Data Migration** (Weeks 5-12)
- Current product data extraction
- Data cleansing and normalization
- Data import to new platform
- Data validation and reconciliation

**Phase 3: Integration** (Weeks 8-16)
- Shopify integration configuration (parallel to migration)
- Data sync setup and testing
- Multi-channel integration (if applicable)
- Error handling and alerting setup

**Phase 4: Testing & Launch** (Weeks 16-20)
- User acceptance testing
- Performance testing and optimization
- Team training
- Go-live and cutover

**Timeline**: X weeks total (typically 5-6 months for mid-size implementations)

#### Section 5: Cost Analysis (½ page)

**Software Licensing**:
- Platform license: $X/month
- User seats: $X/month
- Professional services: $X (one-time)
- **Monthly recurring**: $X
- **Year 1 total**: $X

**Implementation Costs**:
- Vendor implementation: $X
- Integrations and custom development: $X
- Data migration services: $X
- Training and documentation: $X
- **Total implementation**: $X

**Ongoing Operational Costs**:
- Internal team (headcount): X FTE at $X/year
- Maintenance and updates: $X/year
- Performance optimization: $X/year
- **Total annual operational**: $X

**Total Cost of Ownership (3-year)**:
- Year 1: $X (implementation + licensing + operations)
- Year 2: $X (licensing + operations)
- Year 3: $X (licensing + operations)
- **3-Year Total**: $X
- **Annual Average**: $X

**ROI & Payback**:
- Expected business benefits (improved time-to-market, data quality, efficiency)
- Estimated cost savings (reduce manual work, errors, etc.)
- Payback period: X months

#### Section 6: Integration Plan (½ page)

**Shopify Integration**:
- Integration pattern: [PIM as system of record | Hybrid with selective sync | Other]
- Native app or custom integration: [Description]
- Data sync frequency: [Real-time | Hourly | Daily]
- One-way or bi-directional sync: [Direction]
- Key data entities: [Products, variants, inventory, prices, localization, etc.]

**Multi-Channel Integration** (if applicable):
- Marketplace integrations: [Which platforms]
- B2B/wholesale integration: [How will it work]
- External system integrations: [CRM, marketing tools, etc.]

**Data Sync SLA**:
- Expected sync latency: X minutes/hours
- Sync reliability target: >99%
- Error notification: [Automated alert, daily report, etc.]
- Reconciliation process: [How data discrepancies resolved]

#### Section 7: Risk Management (½ page)

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Data migration incomplete/inaccurate | Medium | High | Staged migration, validation reports, rollback plan |
| Integration reliability issues | Low | High | Thorough testing, monitoring, alert setup |
| Team adoption resistance | Medium | Medium | Training program, change management, phased rollout |
| Vendor stability or product direction | Low | High | Vendor financial review, feature roadmap alignment |

#### Section 8: Success Criteria & Metrics (½ page)

**Implementation Success**:
- Platform goes live on schedule (within 1 month of planned date)
- 95%+ of products migrated with valid data
- Zero critical integration failures in first month

**Business Success (6 months post-launch)**:
- Data quality score improves by 30% (fewer incomplete products)
- Time to market for new products reduced by 50%
- Team satisfaction with product data management: 4/5 or higher
- Multi-channel product sync automation: >95% of products

**Technical Success**:
- Integration sync success rate: >99%
- Data latency: <1 hour
- System availability: >99.9%
- No critical data discrepancies between systems

#### Section 9: Recommendations & Next Steps (½ page)

**If Proceeding with Recommended Platform**:
1. Secure executive sponsorship and budget approval
2. Issue RFP or finalize vendor contract
3. Assign project lead and implementation team
4. Schedule vendor kickoff (target: X date)
5. Begin detailed data audit and cleansing (parallel to vendor setup)

**If Choosing Different Platform**:
- [Document reasoning and criteria]
- [Address any concerns or gaps in analysis]

**If Proceeding with No-PIM Approach**:
1. Plan for near-term growth and reassess in 12-24 months
2. Implement product data governance within Shopify
3. Document current data quality baseline
4. Plan for PIM migration when complexity justifies it

## Inputs

- PIM complexity score (Step 2)
- Platform evaluation scorecard (Step 3)
- Integration assessment (Step 4)
- Business profile and requirements (Step 1)

## Outputs

- PIM Selection Recommendation document (5-8 pages)
- Implementation timeline and milestones
- Cost analysis and business case
- Risk register and mitigation strategies
- Integration architecture diagram

## Completion Criteria

- Final platform recommendation clearly justified
- Implementation plan realistic and detailed
- Cost analysis includes all categories (license, implementation, operations)
- ROI and business case documented
- Integration approach clear and feasible
- Risks identified and mitigations proposed
- Executive and technical stakeholder sign-off
- Ready to proceed to detailed implementation planning
